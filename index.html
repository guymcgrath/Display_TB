DisplayTB

This mapping dashboard was created by CVERA, UCD https://www.ucd.ie/cvera/ as part of a research program into Bovine Tuberculosis (bTB) being conducted by the Centre for the Department of Agriculture, Food and the Marine (DAFM). CVERA have created this interface to allow stakeholders to view the current and recent bTB status of herds by area of interest and explore animal movement in and out of these area by source area risk. Please note, this is an ongoing research project and the content is likely to change over time.

The values presented are compiled by CVERA from data snapshots provided by DAFM (from AIM, AHCS and LPIS). This may result in some errors of interpretation and may not account for error-correction in the source data held by DAFM. These dashboards are a research prototype and should not be used for official reporting purposes.

To protect the identity of individual farmers, a process was developed to aggregate farms into larger spatial units. A conventional grid square approach is unsuitable for this because of the variation in farm sizes across the country. Instead, a new spatial aggregation methodology was devised. The centroid of the largest fragment of land for each farm in Ireland was generated (for all active farms having livestock from January 2020 to December 2025). From this, all area in Ireland was split up evenly between these centroids through a process called Tessellation. This creates Thiessen polygons for every farm (Figure 1).

Figure 1. Sample of Thiessen polygons created around the centroid of the largest fragment of land per farm for all farms in Ireland (herds classed as active from January 2020 to December 2025).

 

These synthetic farm shapes were then merged into larger areas ensuring that there were at least 5 farms in each shape (using GHS-SmartDissolve https://publications.jrc.ec.europa.eu/repository/handle/JRC122055 ) (Figure 2).

 

Figure 2. Herds collapsed into spatial units ensuring 5 or more herds for each unit.

 

The bTB herd histories were then summarised for each herd into the spatial unit in which they were assigned.

The dashboard is located at:

https://www.arcgis.com/apps/dashboards/9306f7eb60b1401a8b18f76f61f20aea

This dashboard can be viewed both in Desktop mode and Mobile mode however, most functionality is only available in full Desktop mode.

In Desktop Mode:

The map window displays new breakdowns in the last 12 months for the current yearly quarter. The dashboard items show the values of new breakdowns in the last 12 months and new breakdowns in the last 3 months with additional graphs showing the number of reactors (positive on skin and blood test) over the last 5 years, the number of badger interventions (cull, vaccination and cull + vaccination) and the number of cattle that moved into the area based on the area risk category of where they came from in 2023 (Figure 3).

The dashboard values update to reflect the extent of the map window (that is, they update with zooming to represent the values in the area on the map screen). An individual area can be selected to display detailed information including the number of herds in that spatial unit, the number of animals, the bTB testing information, the movement information (for 2024) and the badger activity. A rectangular selection can also be made to select one or more area units with the dashboard reflecting the selection. The selection must be removed to go back to viewing the values in the map window extent. The background basemaps can be changed from imagery to roads/place maps and the foreground bTB maps can be turned on or off. Addresses can be looked up in the search window, as can EIRCODE. A sidebar can be accessed to the left of the screen giving options to query the data. These queries create new selections visible in the map. Selections include past reactor/lesions information, population and County.

Figure 3. Area Risk generated using an Emerging Hot Spot Analysis (Space Time Pattern Mining) on the 10 year reactor rate of herds in each Thiessen polygon.

 

DisplayTB: Trends and Movement Explorer

Risk areas can be accessed through the accompanying Dashboard at:

https://ucdirelandeu.maps.arcgis.com/apps/dashboards/d392791a112f4d25a0a66758fdb17798

This dashboard is intended for desktop use as there are too many items to display in mobile mode. The dashboard displays the number of movements into and out of selected areas. The movements are by herd, by number of cattle and whether the moves were via a mart or directly from farm to farm. Currently the movement data are from 2024. To select an area, use the selection tool on the top right of the map window. Drag the selection over the area of interest. If zoomed in, the movement lines take a long time to draw; zoom back out and it should refresh. The servers are currently unable to process so many lines at this resolution. The data in the dashboard updates to reflect the selection made or to the extent the map window is zoomed to. The high number of herds prior to making a selection is due to the fact that herds buy in from multiple herds and this is a count of that.

The risk colours reflect a simplified version of an emerging hotpot analysis.

https://pro.arcgis.com/en/pro-app/3.5/tool-reference/space-time-pattern-mining/emerginghotspots.htm

The seventeen risk categories have been collapsed into 3 simplified risk categories. The High Risk category includes areas where bTB is persistently bad, bad and getting worse or is a newly emerging hotpot. The Medium risk category includes where bTB rates are inconsistent and fluctuate over time. The Low risk category includes areas where bTB is low and steadily low, low and improving, no statistical significance detected or bTB free. These data are based on bTB breakdown rates per Thiessen polygon per year from 2016 to 2024. This will be updates in April 2026 to reflect the period 2017 to 2025.

A side bar can be opened which shows all the individual risk categories.
